OWS DISCHARGE PROCEDURE

1. Confirm vessel is en route and outside restricted area
2. Check bilge tank levels
3. Verify OWS and OCM operational
4. Confirm correct valve lineup
5. Start OWS and monitor ppm continuously
6. Stop immediately if ppm fluctuates
7. Record discharge in Oil Record Book

NOTES:
- Never bypass OCM
- Always verify overboard valve physically

---

FIRE MAIN LOW PRESSURE CHECK

1. Verify fire pump is running correctly
2. Check suction valves fully open
3. Check sea chest and strainers for blockage
4. Inspect system for leaks
5. Confirm correct pump selected (main vs emergency)
6. Check pressure at multiple hydrants

NOTES:
- Air in system can reduce pressure
- Blocked strainers common after yard periods

---

EMERGENCY FIRE PUMP TEST

1. Stop main fire pump
2. Start emergency fire pump
3. Verify independent operation
4. Check pressure at hydrants
5. Confirm suction supply available

NOTES:
- Must work with engine room unavailable
- Test under realistic conditions

---

GARBAGE MANAGEMENT PROCEDURE

1. Segregate waste (plastics, food, general)
2. Store plastics for shore disposal
3. Confirm vessel position before discharge
4. Discharge food waste only within limits
5. Record in Garbage Record Book

NOTES:
- Double-check no plastics mixed
- Special areas stricter rules apply
